How to Create and Sell an Online Course Using AI in 2026

Back to all guides

An online course is one of the highest-margin businesses you can build. Create it once and sell it forever — no inventory, no delivery, no time-for-money trap. AI makes it possible to build a professional course in a fraction of the time it used to take.

Why Courses Are the Perfect AI Business

Before AI, building a quality online course took 3–6 months of writing, recording, editing, and designing. With AI tools, that timeline compresses to 2–4 weeks without sacrificing quality. This means you can validate faster, earn sooner, and iterate based on real student feedback.

Step 1 — Choose a Profitable Topic

The best course topics combine three things: something you know well, something others want to learn, and something people will pay money to learn. You do not need to be a world-class expert — you just need to be significantly further along than your target student.

Use ChatGPT to brainstorm: "Give me 10 online course ideas based on the skill of [your background]. For each, describe the target student, the main promise, and a rough 6-module outline."

Step 2 — Validate Before You Build

Before spending weeks creating content, validate that people will actually pay for it. Post in 3 Facebook groups or subreddits where your target audience hangs out. Describe the course and its outcome. If 10+ people express interest or ask how to sign up, you have validation. If nobody responds, adjust your topic or audience.

The Pre-Sale Method

The most powerful validation is taking payment before the course is built. Offer a "founding member" price (30–50% off the regular price) and promise delivery in 4 weeks. If 5–10 people pay, you have a real business — not a theory.

Step 3 — Build the Course with AI

  1. Prompt ChatGPT to create a full 6-module curriculum with lessons, learning objectives, and key takeaways for each module.
  2. For each lesson, prompt: "Write a complete script for a 10-minute video lesson on [topic] for an audience of [target student]. Make it conversational and easy to follow."
  3. Use Canva AI to design your slide deck. Their AI can generate slide layouts from a text prompt in minutes.
  4. Record using Loom (free) or your smartphone in a well-lit room. Audio quality matters more than video quality — use a \$30 USB microphone if possible.
  5. Use Descript (AI editing tool) to remove filler words and silences automatically. Edit in a fraction of the normal time.

How to Market Your Course Without a Big Audience

Most first-time course creators worry that they have no audience. Here is the truth: you do not need one to make your first 10–20 sales. Start with these tactics:

  • Post 3 short videos per week on TikTok or YouTube Shorts teaching one tip from your course. End each video with "more in my full course — link in bio."
  • Share your founding member offer in Facebook groups and Reddit communities in your niche.
  • Email your existing contacts — friends, colleagues, former clients. Even 200 personal contacts can generate 5–10 sales from a genuine recommendation.
  • Use AI to write 5 guest blog posts for websites in your niche. Include a mention of your course in each author bio.
